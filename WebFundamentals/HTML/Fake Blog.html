Can't Out Ramp The Jade

Jade Druids have always been at the top of the Meta in Hearthstone, and with the addition of Ultimate Infestation and Spreading Plague in the latest expansion, their ramp has become even more absurd. Not even Mages, capable of ignoring taunt protections and clearing the board with ease, are safe from the Rampage. Perhaps this was Blizzard's intention, however, for what online game survives without press, and no press is freer than that salty newbies. One day I shall reach the turn 15 30/30 statue without my opponent conceding. It is my destiny.

Required Cards

  • Innervate
  • Jade Idol
  • Gadgetzan Auctioneer
  • Ultimate Infestation
  • Nourish
  • Spreading Plague

Empty Deck Cycling Procedure (Assuming Empty Board)

Mana Left Play Cards in Deck Jade Size (Relative to Start)
10 Gadgetzan Auctioneer 1 +0/+0
4 Jade Idol (shuffle) 3 +0/+0
3 Jade Idol (summon) 2 +1/+1
2 Jade Idol (summon) 1 +2/+2
0 Jade Idol (shuffle) 2 +2/+2
2 Innervate 1 +2/+2
1 Jade Idol (shuffle) 2 +2/+2
0 Jade Idol (shuffle) 4 +2/+2
2 Innervate 3 +2/+2
1 Jade Idol (summon) 2 +3/+3
0 Jade Idol (summon) 1 +4/+4

This hyper cycling will allow you to fill your hand with Jade Idols as well as bump their stats by 4 when you start summoning them the next turn. The only potential drawback to this is you are left with 1 card in your deck, which you will draw your next turn, meaning failing to kill off your Auctionner before casting them again will result in fatigue damage. This is rarely a problem, as your opponent will likely want to kill it off for you to avoid more cycling.
